TODO Eigenvectors and Eigenvalues

Lv3

An eigenvector is a vector that is scaled by multiplication with a matrix. \[ \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 3 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 9 \\ -3 \end{bmatrix} = 3 \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{bmatrix} \]

Suppose we have an eigenvector \(\vec{v}\), and it is scaled by a factor of \(\lambda\). We know that: \[ A\vec{v} = \lambda \vec{v} \]

From this we derive: \[ (A - \lambda I) \vec{v} = \vec{0} \]

TODO Eigenspaces

Lv3

An eigenspace of an eigenvalue is the span of the eigenvectors corresponding to that value \(\lambda\), denoted by \[ E_{\lambda} = \Nul(A - \lambda I) \]

TODO Characteristic Equations

Lv3

The polynomial \(\det(A - \lambda I)\) is the characteristic polynomial of \(A\) and by solving this for zero, we get the characteristic equation. It tells us how to find eigenvalues.

TODO Matrix Similarity

A similarity transformation is actually a change of basis.

Lv3

If \(A\) and \(B\) are two \(n \times n\) matrices, then \(A\) is similar to \(B\) iff there is an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(B = P^{-1}AP\). This transformation is a similarity transformation.

Similar matrices have the same characteristic equation and eigenvalues. However, the reverse is not true!

TODO Diagonalisation

Lv3

An \(n \times n\) matrix is diagonalisable if it is similar to a diagonal matrix.

TODO Orthogonality

Does this really belong in this section?

Orthogonality is an abstraction over the concept of 'perpendicular-ness'.

Lv3

  • The inner product of two vectors is defined as \[< \vec{v}, \vec{u} > = \sum_{j=1}^n u_j \overline{v}_j \]
  • Two vectors are orthogonal if \(< \vec{v}, \vec{u} > = 0\).
  • A set of vectors is orthogonal if every vector in the set is orthogonal to every other vector (pairwise orthogonal).
